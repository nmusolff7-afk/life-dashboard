How we calculate your targets

These targets are derived from peer-reviewed sports nutrition research, not AI guesswork. Every number traces back to a formula you can verify.

Protein by goal
Protein need scales with metabolic stress, not growth. The more catabolic pressure your body is under, the more protein you need to preserve muscle.
Lose weight 2.0 g/kg — mid-range of 1.8–2.4 g/kg shown to preserve lean mass during caloric restriction (Helms et al. 2014) Body Recomposition 2.2 g/kg — highest of the four goals; simultaneous fat loss + muscle gain requires maximum protein to fuel synthesis while in a mild deficit Build muscle 1.8 g/kg — a caloric surplus is already anabolic, so protein needs are lower; above ~1.6 g/kg shows diminishing returns for hypertrophy (Morton et al. 2018) Maintain 1.6 g/kg — roughly 2x the RDA; sufficient to maintain muscle mass without aggressive body composition goals
Calorie target
Your total daily energy expenditure (TDEE) is adjusted by goal: lose −20%, recomp −10%, maintain 0%, build +10%. The target never drops below your resting metabolic rate.
Fat
25% of target calories or 0.7 g/kg body weight, whichever is higher. The floor protects hormone production and nutrient absorption.
Carbs
Whatever calories remain after protein and fat, divided by 4. Minimum 100g/day to support brain function and training intensity.
Sources: Helms et al. (2014) J Int Soc Sports Nutr · Morton et al. (2018) Br J Sports Med · Mifflin et al. (1990) Am J Clin Nutr · Katch & McArdle (1996)
