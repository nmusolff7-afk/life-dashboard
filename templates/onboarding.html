There are three proven ways to get lean and muscular. Each has trade-offs.
Cut first, then bulk
Recommended if BF% > 18%
Pros Faster visible results. Easier to gauge muscle gain later when you're leaner. Insulin sensitivity improves, making a future bulk more efficient.
Cons You won't gain muscle during the cut. Requires patience to see the full transformation across two phases.
Apex sets you up with a fat loss phase first.
Bulk first, then cut
Recommended if BF% < 15%
Pros Maximizes muscle growth rate. Strength gains come faster. More food makes training easier and recovery better.
Cons You'll gain some fat alongside muscle. You'll need a cut phase afterward to reveal the muscle you built.
Apex sets you up with a muscle gain phase first.
Recomposition
Best for beginners or returning lifters
Pros No distinct phases — your body composition improves gradually. No bulk/cut cycles. Psychologically easier to sustain long-term.
Cons Slowest path. Results take longer to see. Requires very consistent protein intake and training. Less effective if you're already lean and experienced.
Apex sets you up with a recomposition plan — mild deficit, highest protein.
